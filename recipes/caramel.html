Caramel Candy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Butter
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 cups light corn syrup
  • 24 ounces evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Sea Salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Line a 9x13" pan with parchment paper.
  2. Add butter, sugar and corn syrup to a large saucepan. Stir over medium heat until mixture begins to boil, about 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add evaporated milk extremely gradually, patience is important. It may take up to 15 minutes to add a single can of milk. Stir contantly, make sure a steady heat is maintained.
  4. Stir mixture constantly, while also scraping the sides. Do this until your reach 240-245 degrees F. Double check with the Ice Water Method. See Below.
  5. Remove from heat and add vanilla.
  6. Pour caramels into pan (Add sea salt if desired) and refrigerate for at least a few hours but overnight is preferable. This makes it easier to cut and wrap if desired. (They will be hard in the frige but soft at room temperature.)

Notes

  • Use a heavy bottom saucepan with a lot of space in case the carmel bubbles. Avoid non-stick.
  • Make sure there are no impurities in your sugar, pan, or spatula. Remove sugar lumps.
  • If your carmel is too soft it needs to cook longer
  • If your carmel is too hard it cooked for too long
  • Ice Water Method: instead of using a candy thermometer you can test if your carmel is ready with some ice water. Spoon some carmel into a cup of ice water and attempt to mold the carmel into a ball. If it is firm, pliable, and sticky it is ready.
  • Carmel may begin to foam, this is normal.
